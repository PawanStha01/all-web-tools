About Age Calculation

Age calculation is the process of determining the elapsed time between a person's birth date and the current date (or another specified date). While it seems simple, accurate age calculation involves several complexities:

Different Ways to Calculate Age
  • Calendar Age: The most common method, counting years, months, and days since birth.
  • Lunar Age: Used in some cultures, based on lunar calendar cycles.
  • Developmental Age: Used in pediatrics to assess a child's development relative to peers.
Interesting Age Facts
  • In some East Asian cultures, a person is considered 1 year old at birth and gains another year on New Year's Day rather than on their birthday.
  • The legal age for various activities (voting, driving, drinking) varies widely across different countries.
  • Leap year babies (born on February 29) technically only have a birthday every four years.
  • The oldest documented person in history lived to 122 years and 164 days.
Note

This calculator uses the Gregorian calendar for all calculations. For legal purposes, age calculations may vary based on local laws and regulations.
